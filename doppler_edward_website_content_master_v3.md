# Expertise

My expertise is not software implementation in the generic sense. I focus on the commercial and financial logic that enterprise systems must execute: pricing conditions, promotional mechanics, funding logic, accruals, claims, deductions, settlement, master data, and Gross-to-Net integrity.

A platform can only execute what the business has made clear. When the business logic is unresolved, the system does not fix it. It exposes it.

Commercial execution is the operating layer between strategy and results. It is where customer agreements, pricing rules, trade spend, promotional events, order behavior, and financial postings either align or start quietly breaking the business.

### I work on:

- Commercial operating model design
- Trade governance
- Approval rules and decision rights
- Promotional policy and guardrails
- Funding logic
- Event-level profitability
- Claims and settlement behavior
- Controls and auditability

Revenue Growth Management only works when planning, execution, settlement, and measurement close the loop. TPM and TPO platforms can help, but only if the commercial model, data structure, workflow, and ERP consequences are understood before the system hardens.

### I work on:

- RGM operating model review
- TPM/TPO design validation
- Promotional planning processes

# Writing / Field Notes

I write about commercial execution, enterprise systems, finance, AI, and the strange ways organizations turn simple decisions into expensive machinery.

The writing is practitioner-led. It comes from operating experience, not abstract commentary. The themes are consistent: commercial intent, system behavior, financial consequences, incentives, governance, and the gap between what people say a process does and what it actually does under pressure.

---

## Themes

### Commercial Intent

Commercial decisions are easy to discuss and hard to execute. The real question is whether a customer agreement, promotional mechanic, or pricing decision survives the enterprise without becoming financially distorted.

### RGM / TPM Reality

RGM and TPM platforms do not magically create discipline. They amplify the logic, data, governance, and incentives already present in the business. If those are broken, the system becomes a very expensive mirror.

### Trade Governance

Trade spend without governance becomes leakage. Governance is not bureaucracy for its own sake. It is the operating discipline that determines who can make commercial decisions, under what conditions, with what evidence, and with what financial accountability.

### ERP-Centric Commercial Finance

ERP is where commercial optimism goes to be audited. The system has to post, settle, reconcile, and report what the business agreed to do. If the logic is wrong upstream, the financial outcome will be wrong downstream.

### AI and Decision-Making

AI can accelerate analysis and education. It can also manufacture confidence, hide assumptions, and make weak thinking look polished. The question is not whether AI is impressive. The question is whether it improves the decision.

### Teaching and Simulation

People learn business by making decisions, seeing consequences, and understanding the system they are operating in. Simulations can make that possible without detonating a real P&L.

---

## Featured Essay Concepts

These can become blog posts, LinkedIn articles, or short field notes.

### Commercial Intent Must Survive the Enterprise

A commercial agreement is not complete when the deal is signed. It is complete when the system can execute it, the customer can be billed correctly, deductions can be resolved, settlement can be controlled, and Finance can trust the P&L.

---

### Before Design Comes Readiness

Most design failures are readiness failures discovered too late. Before a TPM or RGM design phase begins, the business needs clarity on commercial logic, master data, ERP integration, decision rights, stakeholder incentives, and the definition of success.

---

### The Platform Is Not the Operating Model

A platform can support an operating model. It cannot invent one. When companies deploy software without clear governance, they do not get transformation. They get old behavior in a new interface.

---

### UAT Is Not a Dumpster for Unresolved Business Logic

UAT should validate design. It should not become a late-stage discovery process for broken master data, undefined pricing logic, unclear ownership, and unresolved commercial disagreements.

---

### Change Management Is Governance

Change management does not begin two weeks before go-live. It begins at Day 0, when sponsors define what behavior will change, what leadership will reinforce, what exceptions will be tolerated, and what the new operating model is expected to govern.

---

### AI Is Not Strategy

AI can help people reason, summarize, simulate, and test assumptions. It cannot replace commercial judgment, operating discipline, data quality, or decision rights. The future belongs to people who know where AI helps and where it becomes a fog machine.

# RGM / TPM Practitioner Briefing

RGM is not a dashboard. TPM is not a workflow. TPO is not a magic pricing oracle.

These systems only work when commercial logic, data readiness, ERP integration, decision rights, user incentives, and financial controls align.

Most failed RGM and TPM programs do not fail because the software cannot do anything useful. They fail because the business has not made the underlying operating logic clear enough for the system to execute.

---

## What RGM / TPM Is Trying to Accomplish

A good RGM / TPM environment bridges what was negotiated with a customer and what actually gets executed in the market.

It must capture promotion mechanics, funding allocations, performance expectations, approval rules, customer structures, product structures, pricing logic, accrual behavior, settlement paths, and financial outcomes.

The chain looks simple:

Commercial Logic  
→ Execution  
→ Accruals / Settlement  
→ Revenue Plan  
→ P&L / Balance Sheet

The reality is not simple.

Every arrow is a failure point.

---

## What Breaks

### Commercial Logic Is Unresolved

The business cannot clearly explain how promotions are planned, approved, funded, executed, settled, and measured.

### Data Is Assumed Ready

Customer hierarchies, product hierarchies, pricing conditions, and historical baselines are treated as available and reliable until UAT proves otherwise.

### ERP Is Treated as Downstream

ERP integration is treated as a technical detail instead of a design input. That is how design choices become financial defects.

### Stakeholders Are Politely Misaligned

Sales, Finance, IT, and leadership appear aligned until a real trade-off is required.

### Success Is Undefined

Go-live happens, but no one agrees whether the project actually worked.

---

## Pre-Design Readiness

Before design begins, the business needs clarity in six domains.

### 01 — Commercial Operating Model

How are promotions planned, approved, executed, and settled today? Who owns decisions? What trade events exist? What must change?

### 02 — Data Readiness

Are customer hierarchies, product hierarchies, pricing conditions, historical baselines, and sell-in / sell-through / sell-out data usable?

### 03 — ERP Integration Landscape

Which ERP modules are in scope? What must the TPM-to-ERP flow support? Where are manual steps today? Who owns the integration layer?

### 04 — Stakeholder and Incentive Alignment

Are Sales, Finance, IT, and leadership aligned on goals, trade-offs, definitions, and what success actually means?

### 05 — Current-State Process Reality

What does the business actually do today, not what it says it does in a workshop?

### 06 — Success Metrics and Definition of Done

How will value be measured? What is the baseline? Who signs off that the outcome was achieved?

---

## The Advisor Role in Design

The advisor role is not project management, configuration coordination, or vendor apologetics.

The role is the commercial logic guardian.

The advisor ensures the design reflects how the client’s business actually operates, not how a platform template assumes it operates.

### The advisor must challenge:

- Whether the as-is process is real or aspirational
- Whether a gap is genuine, misunderstood, or configurable
- Whether the proposed design will work downstream in ERP
- Whether financial outcomes will be accurate and auditable
- Whether workarounds are being accepted without ownership
- Whether UAT scenarios cover real commercial edge cases
- Whether sign-off reflects understanding or fatigue

### One question matters:

Does this design reflect the commercial intent of the business, or does it reflect the default behavior of the platform?

If it reflects commercial intent, proceed.

If it reflects platform default, challenge it.

If no one knows, stop and clarify before the ambiguity becomes expensive.

---

## UAT and Defect Validation

UAT is where bad assumptions send their invoices.

A strong UAT process does not just test standard flows. It tests commercial edge cases, financial postings, accrual behavior, settlement paths, claims behavior, master data dependencies, and user decisions under realistic pressure.

Defect validation matters because not every issue is a bug.

An issue may be:

- A system bug
- A data issue
- A design flaw
- A configuration choice
- A change request
- A process misunderstanding
- A training issue
- An adoption failure
- A commercial logic problem

Those are different problems. Treating them the same is how projects lose time, credibility, and money.

---

## Change Management

Change management begins at Day 0.

It is not communications. It is governance.

A TPM implementation changes how people plan, approve, fund, execute, settle, measure, and explain commercial activity. That affects Sales, Finance, IT, Operations, leadership, and sometimes external partners.

### Prepare

Build clarity, sponsorship, and behavioral readiness before the system hardens.

- Stakeholder mapping
- Sponsor engagement
- Change impact by role
- Communication plan
- Training approach
- Design principles
- Champion network

### Enable

Move users from awareness to confidence.

- Key user training
- UAT as ownership-building
- Resistance management
- Pilot approach where possible
- Executive communication
- User readiness assessment

### Sustain

Prevent regression into old habits.

- Hypercare
- Workaround elimination
- Adoption quality measurement
- Governance ownership
- Dispute resolution
- Feedback loops
- Recognition of early adopters

A go-live is not the end of the change. It is the beginning of the hardest part.
