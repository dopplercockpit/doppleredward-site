Finance decisions under real operating constraints.

Redline is a simulation engine for finance decisions under real operating constraints. It puts students, managers, and teams inside a live business environment where pricing decisions, supplier terms, customer contracts, demand shifts, inventory, cash flow, and risk all interact.

No multiple-choice fantasy.

No clean case study with the answer hidden in paragraph four. Every decision has consequences.

What it is

An interactive CFO office.

Participants run a fictional company through scenarios involving commercial, financial, and operational pressure. The objective is to teach financial decision-making, not spreadsheet key-mashing.

PricingDiscountsVolumeSupplier termsWorking capitalInventoryCustomer contractsCash flowRiskCapital allocation
Why I built it

Traditional finance education often teaches concepts in isolation. Real business does not work that way.

A price cut affects volume, margin, cash, operations, customer behavior, and future expectations. A supplier concession may improve short-term liquidity while increasing long-term risk. A volume commitment can look profitable until capacity, inventory, or working capital tells a different story.

Universities and Business Schools

Courses in FP&A, finance, accounting, strategy, operations, commercial finance, and RGM.

Corporate Finance and Commercial Teams

Training for analysts, managers, and cross-functional teams that need to understand financial consequences under pressure.

Executive Education

Scenario-based learning for leaders who need to practice decisions before those decisions become real.
