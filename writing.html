Themes

The recurring arguments.

Commercial Intent

Commercial decisions are easy to discuss and hard to execute. The real question is whether the customer agreement, promotional mechanic, or pricing decision survives the enterprise.

RGM / TPM Reality

RGM and TPM platforms amplify the logic, data, governance, and incentives already present in the business. If those are broken, the system becomes an expensive mirror.

Trade Governance

Trade spend without governance becomes leakage. Governance determines who can make commercial decisions, with what evidence, and with what financial accountability.

ERP-Centric Commercial Finance

ERP is where commercial optimism goes to be audited. The system has to post, settle, reconcile, and report what the business agreed to do.

AI and Decision-Making

AI can accelerate analysis and education. It can also manufacture confidence, hide assumptions, and make weak thinking look polished.

Teaching and Simulation

People learn business by making decisions, seeing consequences, and understanding the system they are operating in.

Essay concepts

Field notes in development.

Commercial Intent Must Survive the Enterprise

A commercial agreement is complete when the system can execute it, the customer can be billed correctly, settlement can be controlled, and Finance can trust the P&L.

Before Design Comes Readiness

Most design failures are readiness failures discovered too late.

The Platform Is Not the Operating Model

A platform can support an operating model. It cannot invent one.

UAT Is Not a Dumpster for Unresolved Business Logic

UAT should validate design, not become late-stage discovery for broken master data and undefined pricing logic.

Change Management Is Governance

Change begins at Day 0, when sponsors define what behavior will change and what leadership will reinforce.

AI Is Not Strategy

AI can help people reason, summarize, simulate, and test assumptions. It cannot replace commercial judgment or operating discipline.
