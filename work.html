CORE CAPABILITIES

01

RESCUE & REMEDIATION

STATUS: ACTIVE

Most SaaS deployments fail because they ignore the human workflow and the real operating model.

We deploy as a fractional Chief RGM / FP&A authority to triage broken implementations. We audit configuration, interrogate data flows, and force alignment between Finance, Sales, and IT.

Then we re-engineer the end-to-end process until the tool actually delivers the ROI that was promised in the board deck.

Typical triggers:

  • TPM/RGM project stuck in "pilot" or perpetual hypercare
  • Low adoption, conflicting numbers, or firefights before business reviews
  • Trade spend, deductions, and "Gross-to-Net" leakage nobody can fully explain
02

FUNCTIONAL DEPLOYMENT

STATUS: ACTIVE

System integrators know the code. We know the trade desk, the P&L, and the retailer reality.

We sit on the client side during global rollouts, managing the unsexy but critical work: Price Pack Architecture, Gross-to-Net settlement, funding logic, and UAT pre-flight checks.

Our job is to ensure the software survives contact with the market and with your internal politics – not just that it passes a technical go-live.

Typical triggers:

  • Multi-country TPM / Demand Planning rollout (EMEA, NA, LATAM, etc.)
  • Need for a "single truth" across Sales, Finance, and Supply Chain
  • Fear that the tool will revert to offline Excel the minute the SI leaves
03

VENDOR INTERROGATION

STATUS: ACTIVE

We run bare-knuckle fit–gap assessments for boards, CFOs, and PE sponsors.

Before you commit to a multi-million-dollar platform, we interrogate the functional limits of tools like Visualfabriq, SAP, UpClear, and others – from the standpoint of RGM, Finance, and trade operations, not just architecture.

We separate genuine predictive capability from marketing theater, and we stress-test whether the platform can survive your data, your retailers, and your internal process gap.

Typical triggers:

  • RFP shortlists where the slideware all looks the same
  • PE or corporate DD evaluating an RGM/TPM asset
  • Concern that "AI" claims are just regression models wrapped in buzzwords
