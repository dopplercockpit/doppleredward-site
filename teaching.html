Teaching philosophy

People learn business by making decisions and seeing consequences.

My teaching focuses on practical decision-making, not abstract theory. Students need to understand how financial logic, commercial behavior, data, systems, and incentives collide under pressure.

I design coursework that asks students to reason from constraints, test assumptions, explain trade-offs, and understand why the system responds the way it does.

That is also why simulation matters. A good learning environment lets people practice judgment before the stakes become real.
